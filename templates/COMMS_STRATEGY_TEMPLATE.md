# Product comms strategy — template

> Fill this file in BEFORE launching ads. The media-buyer agent reads it to
> write offers and copy in your voice and to avoid breaking brand taboos.
>
> This is a generic blank template. Copy it for your product:
> `cp COMMS_STRATEGY_TEMPLATE.md ../comms-<your-product>.md` and fill it in.
>
> Fields in `<angle brackets>` — replace them. Whatever doesn't apply — delete it, don't leave it blank.

---

## 1. Product in one sentence

<What it is and who it's for. No fluff. Example: "A web app that turns family
stories into a printed book in one evening.">

## 2. Who we're selling to (ICP)

- **Who:** <age, role, situation. Example: 30-50, parents, value family memories>
- **Pain:** <what problem the product solves>
- **Buying trigger:** <what makes them buy right now>
- **Where they are in Meta:** <Instagram/Facebook, which interests as HINTS — not as hard targeting>
- **Geo and language:** <country/countries, ad account currency, ad language>

> ⚠️ Don't mix this up: geo and currency affect ALL kill/scale thresholds (see `knowledge/06`).
> Narrow interest targeting is outdated — interests are hints only (see `knowledge/02`).

## 3. Offer (Hormozi-style)

A strong offer = specifics + a tangible benefit + honest urgency + risk reversal.

- **Main benefit:** <not a feature, but a result for the person>
- **Value anchor:** <what we compare the price against, what's included>
- **Risk reversal:** <guarantee / free step / refund>
- **Honest urgency:** <a real deadline/limit — NOT a forever fake timer>
- **CTA:** <a concrete action. "Get a free 15-min audit," not "Contact us">

Check the offer against the anti-pattern table in `knowledge/03_creatives.md`.

## 4. Tone of voice (communication rules)

- **Voice:** <what kind? friendly/expert/warm/bold — 2-3 adjectives>
- **Point of view:** <"we" / "I" / third person>
- **Language:** <copy language; if the market is bilingual — which one we actually use in the ads>
- **Length:** the offer and the key message — in the first ~125 characters of the primary text (before "See More").

## 5. TABOOS — what we NEVER do in communication

> This is the most important field. Put here everything that destroys trust or contradicts the brand.

- <Forbidden topics. Example: don't mention death/loss — only the positive of preserving>
- <Forbidden tactics. Example: don't use fake urgency>
- <Forbidden phrasings. Example: don't frame the product as a "replacement for people">
- <Sensitive segments we do NOT target>

## 6. Special category?

- [ ] Does the product relate to credit/finance/housing/employment/health?
  If so — targeting by age/gender/geo is restricted by Meta. See `knowledge/05`, rule 5.6.
  The entire audience strategy from file 02 changes. Check this BEFORE launch.

## 7. What counts as a conversion

- **Main event:** <Purchase / Lead / Sign Up / ...>
- **If purchases are <50/week:** which frequent event do we drop down to (the ladder, `knowledge/05`)?
  <View Content / Add to Cart / Initiate Checkout>
- **Target CPA (in the ad account's currency):** <a number — every threshold is derived from it. Not the US median!>

---

### How the agent uses this
1. Before writing copy/offers — it reads this whole file.
2. It takes the voice, taboos, and offer from here; thresholds come from `knowledge/` adjusted for geo (file 06).
3. It never breaks section 5 (TABOOS), even if "that would be more viral."
