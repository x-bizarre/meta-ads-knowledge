# 03 — Creatives and offers (Meta Ads, B2C + traffic, 2025-2026)

---

### Rule 3.1: How many creatives per ad set (ALIGNED with file 01)
- **Gist:** For the Andromeda algorithm — consolidation: fewer ad sets, more creatives in each one.
- **Threshold:** Meta best practice — **10-20 per AD SET** with 1-3 ad sets. An agency starting point for a new account/small budget — **3-6** strong ones, adding more over time. ⚠️ 20+ weak creatives hurt: the algorithm concentrates budget on the leaders, and the extras dilute the learning data.
- **Reality:** only ~2% of creatives become scalable winners; out of ~10 tested, 1-3 are strong.
- **Reliability:** [OFFICIAL META] (10-20) + [AGENCY CONSENSUS] (3-6 to start)

### Rule 3.2: How much data for learning
- **Gist:** An ad set needs ~50 conversions/week. If you spread that across too many creatives, optimization weakens. This is the main argument against overloading.
- **Budget to test a creative:** ~$100-150 [AGENCY CONSENSUS, not a Meta figure]. ⚠️ Lower for non-US geos — see file 06.
- **Reliability:** [OFFICIAL META] (50 conv) + [AGENCY CONSENSUS] ($100-150)

### Rule 3.3: Format — vertical 9:16 is the main one
- **Gist:** The vast majority of inventory and users are mobile. The single format that covers the most placements (Reels, Stories, the mobile feed) is 9:16. Build vertical-first. For static, 4:5 is acceptable.
- ⚠️ The figures "90% of inventory / 98% of users are mobile" are [UNVERIFIED], cited repeatedly without a direct Meta report. The conclusion (vertical-first) holds regardless of the exact %.
- **Reliability:** [AGENCY CONSENSUS]

### Rule 3.4: Video vs Static vs Carousel
- **Video / Reels** — the best reach and engagement, the main driver of 2025-2026.
- **Static** — for direct response (Sales/Leads), often no worse than video: a simple image with a clear value prop + a strong CTA.
- **Carousel** — storytelling, before/after, product bundles for the same price.
- **For traffic/reach** — lean into dynamics and motion; **for conversions** — lean into offer clarity, proof, and CTA.
- **Reliability:** [AGENCY CONSENSUS]

### Rule 3.5: Hook in the first 3 seconds (video)
- **Gist:** The user decides whether to "scroll or watch" within ~3 seconds. Put the brand/product/most striking shot in the first 3 seconds, BEFORE logos. A strong hook = motion, bold text, an unexpected visual, or a direct question/benefit.
- **Captions are mandatory** — a lot of video is watched without sound (do NOT cite the exact "85%" figure — it's a 2016 claim Meta never confirmed). Captions are needed for other reasons: mobile, scroll, accessibility.
- **Reliability:** [AGENCY CONSENSUS] + [OFFICIAL META] (the captions recommendation)

### Rule 3.6: Text length
- **Primary text:** on mobile, ~125 characters before "See More" — put the key message and offer BEFORE the cutoff.
- **Headline:** short, 27 characters for Feed (40 max).
- **Reliability:** [OFFICIAL META] (recommended field limits)

### Rule 3.7: UGC vs polished
- **Gist:** Polished studio ads read instantly as "an ad." UGC looks like an organic post → it slips past the defensive filters. For most DTC/B2C, native UGC outperforms studio content.
- ⚠️ The multipliers "3-5× better," "+44% CTR," "+300%" are [UNVERIFIED], from agency/vendor blogs. The direction is right, the exact numbers aren't. For premium/luxury, studio content may work better.
- **"Creative = 70-80% of the result"** — a strong consensus as a principle, but the exact share comes from vendor materials; treat it as a reference point.
- **Reliability:** [AGENCY CONSENSUS]

### Rule 3.8: Creative testing
- **Gist:** Test new creatives FIRST against each other, then validate against the current leader, then scale alongside it (without replacing the winner). The 80/20 rule: 80% of budget on proven creatives, 20% on tests. Don't clone the winner 20 times — repackage the message into NEW formats.
- **Rotation:** new creatives every 7-10 days at top brands.
- **Reliability:** [AGENCY CONSENSUS]

### Rule 3.9: Creative fatigue
- **Gist:** The fatigue signal is rising frequency + rising CPA/CPM + falling CTR/ROAS. Under Andromeda the fatigue window for cold audiences has narrowed — refresh every 2-3 weeks.
- **Frequency thresholds [agency heuristic, NOT a Meta rule]:** cold <3.0; warm 4-6; awareness 8-10+. ⚠️ These thresholds swing by multiples (see file 04: Meta talks about a plateau at ≈2, but there are working accounts with frequency 10-16). Decide on CTR/CPC/CPA, not on frequency itself.
- ⚠️ "a top creative loses 38% over 5 weeks" — [UNVERIFIED].
- **Reliability:** [AGENCY CONSENSUS / heuristic]

### Rule 3.10: Advantage+ Creative (auto-enhancements)
- **Gist:** AI tools that generate variations and tweak the visual/text. Meta: standard enhancements deliver ~-4% cost per result. BUT for brands with strong ready-made creatives, many enhancements do harm (they break the copy, crop text, add inappropriate music, distort the visual).
- **Safe strategy:** run your own quality creatives, A/B-test 1-2 enhancements selectively rather than "turning everything on."
- **Turn off with care:** Text improvements (breaks the copy), Music/Video filters (random audio), Site links (send people to random pages), Translate text.
- **Reliability:** [OFFICIAL META] (-4%) + [DISPUTED] (what to turn off)

## OFFER ANTI-PATTERNS

A strong offer = specificity + a tangible benefit + honest urgency + risk removal (Hormozi's "Grand Slam Offer"). A weak offer fails more often than a bad product.

| Bad | Why | Better |
|---|---|---|
| Generic name ("Business Coaching Program") | Gets lost in the noise | "Download our free Meta Ads ROI calculator" |
| Vague CTA ("Contact us") | No reason to act now | "Get a free 15-min audit this week" |
| No urgency | The default decision = "never" | Honest: "Enrollment ends Friday" |
| Features instead of benefits ("24/7 support") | A feature alone means nothing | "Get unstuck at 2 AM before launch" |
| A price with no anchor | Compresses perceived value | Value anchor + bonuses BEFORE the price |
| Fake urgency (an eternal timer) | Works short-term, kills trust | Real cohorts, seasonality, limited seats |

- **Reliability:** [AGENCY CONSENSUS] + Hormozi's framework (widely accepted, not "Meta data")

## CONTRADICTIONS AND OPEN QUESTIONS

1. **Creatives per ad set: 10-20 (Meta) vs 3-6 (agencies).** Go by your own budget/conversions, not an abstract number.
2. **Advantage+ Creative "turn everything on" (Meta) vs "turn most of it off" (agencies).** Turn things on selectively and A/B-test.
3. **Polished vs UGC.** UGC dominates for DTC/B2C, but it's not absolute — premium/luxury is different.

## OUTDATED — DO NOT APPLY

- The fragmented structure "5-10 narrow ad sets + 1 creative per ad set" (loses to consolidation).
- The "20% text rule" (max 20% text on an image) — Meta scrapped it in 2020.
- "85% of video is watched without sound" as a fact — a 2016 claim Meta never confirmed (captions are still needed).
- Old pre-COVID CPA/CPM benchmarks — they shifted after iOS 14.5.
